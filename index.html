前言

黎明前的黑暗，921大地震給予人們的創傷，在歷史上留了一道不可抹平的傷疤。一陣巨震，驚醒了在夢鄉的人們，頓時，黑暗來臨，帶給台灣毀滅性的打擊。而黎明再起，堅忍不摧的民族，靠著一絲陽光，如青草般重生。

Introduction

The darkness before dawn. The trauma of the 921 earthquake left an indelible scar in history. An earthquake awakened people in their dreamland. Suddenly, darkness came, bringing a devastating strike to Taiwan. However, the dawn shall prevail. The stoic and unbreakable nationality had been reborn, like grass with a ray of sunlight.

策展論述

由於近期頻繁的地震，除了讓我們陷入意料之外的恐慌，也不禁回想起921地震對台灣的影響。1999年9月21日清晨1時47分，臺灣中部發生芮氏規模7.3的強烈地震，造成的傷亡及財物損失為近百年來臺灣最大的地震災害之一。藉由此次展覽，了解921的起因、災害，以及災後重建，最後提供地震體驗屋的體驗，並希望藉此體驗提升大家的危機意識，不要遺忘這歷史的傷痕。

Curatorial discourse

Due to the recent frequent earthquakes, we fall into unexpected panic. In addition, we can't help but recall the impact of the 921 earthquake on Taiwan. At 1:47 in the morning on September 21, 1999, a 7.3 Richter Scale earthquake occurred in the centre of Taiwan, causing casualties and property losses as one of the largest earthquake disasters in Taiwan in the past century. This exhibition explores the cause, disasters, and reconstruction of the 921 earthquake, and provides an earthquake room experience. Through this exhibition, we hope to enhance people’s awareness of the crisis, and not to forget this historical scar.

一百秒的時光．一輩子的創傷

921大地震，又稱為集集大地震。在1999年9月21日 在凌晨1點47分發生在臺灣中部山區的逆斷層型地震，造成了臺灣全島都感受到嚴重搖晃，仍是自臺灣戰後時期傷亡損失最大的自然災害。震央位於在南投縣集集鎮境內，芮氏規模為7.3。這場地震肇因於車籠埔斷層的錯動，並且在地表造成長達為85公里的破裂帶。
這場地震造成大約有2500人罹難，也震毀了許多道路與橋樑等交通設施、堰壩以及堤防等水利設施，以及電力設備、維生管線、工業設施、醫院設施、學校等公共設施，更引發了大規模的山崩與土壤液化災害，其中又以台灣中部受災最為嚴重。

One hundred second of time, A trauma of lifetime

The 921 earthquake, the second largest quake in recorded history of Taiwan(the most serious one was in 1935) , was also known as the Ji-Ji earthquake. An earthquake with a magnitude of 7.3 occurred in Ji-Ji , Nantou County of Taiwan on 21 September 1999 at 01:47 a.m. The earthquake was caused by the dislocation of the Chelungpu chasm and caused a 85-kilometer rupture area on the surface. According to Wikipedia, the earthquake claimed approximately 2500 people. It also destroyed many roads and bridges and other transportation facilities；weirs and dikes and other water conservancy facilities；electric power equipment, life-sustaining pipelines, industrial facilities, hospital facilities, schools and other public facilities. Moreover, it triggered large-scale landslides and disasters of soil liquefaction, of which central Taiwan was the most affected.

災民訪問

透過訪問災民，他們表示：「當時一個天搖地動，如同砲彈轟炸般的爆炸聲，從睡夢中驚醒的我們，連滾帶爬的衝到街上，整座城市壟罩著黑暗，大樓在面前倒塌，哀嚎聲此起彼落，這一切有如夢魘，揮之不去的恐懼感，在心頭上扎根，害怕得不敢回家裡的我們，只能待在車子上，聽著斷斷續續的廣播，默默地為台灣祈禱。」

Interview of victims

Through interviews with the victims, they said, “ At that time, everything was shaking, and the sound of the explosion suddenly broke out, like a bombardment. We were awakened by the earthquake, and rushed to the street in panic. The whole city was covered with darkness. Suddenly, buildings collapsed in front of us, and everyone wailed with desperation. All this was a nightmare. The lingering sense of fear rooted in our mind. None of us had the courage to go home, and we had no choice but to stay in the car, listening to the intermittent broadcasts, praying for Taiwan.”

災後重建

絕望環繞著，無數的大樓倒塌，但是希望卻不曾消失。「災害發生是危機，災後重建卻有可能是更大的轉機。」，這句話打動了不少民眾，它讓我們知道人類是多麼的脆弱，也讓我們意識到學習與環境相處的重要性。地震是不會向人們妥協的，有許多倒塌的大樓，都是因為偷工減料和耐震規範不嚴格所致，這是921大地震給我們的血淚教訓。所幸，許多民間團體、建築專家積極的投入重建。對於受災地區的重建，給了莫大的幫助，讓人民都能生活在安全的建築裡。多年之後，透過資訊累積及文件紀錄，台灣有豐富的重建經驗可提供接之而來的南亞海嘯、中國四川震災、緬甸洪災…。未來全球氣候變遷，面對嚴苛環境考驗，我們更需要認真看待「環境保護」一事，並融入災後復建以及風險管理的考量與規畫中。

Reconstruction

Watching countless buildings collapsing, people were in the depth of despair ; however, the hope never disappears.
"A disaster is a crisis, but the reconstruction after the disaster could be a bigger turning point." This sentence touched many people. It lets us know how fragile humans are, realizing the importance of learning to get along with the environment. The earthquake never gives in to people. There are many collapsed buildings because of cutting corners and non-strict seismic standards. The 921 earthquake taught us the lesson of blood and tears. Fortunately, many non-governmental organizations and construction experts have actively engaged in reconstruction. Thus, great help was given to the reconstruction of the disaster-stricken areas, so that people can live in safe buildings. After many years, through the accumulation of information and document records, Taiwan has a wealth of reconstruction experience that can provide for the South Asian tsunami, the Sichuan earthquake in China, the floods in Myanmar, etc. In the face of severe environmental challenges in the future due to global climate change, we need to take "environmental protection" seriously and incorporate it into considerations and plans for post-disaster reconstruction and risk management.

素材

策展論述讓參觀者知道因為某個契機，讓我們想重返921的發生背景和過程。再來到第一個展區，我們選擇用照片及文字敘述輔佐的方式呈現這個地震的起因和所造成的災害。接著，我們用一些當時的新聞、報紙、還有廣播的聲音，以及帳篷的布景，來創造出一個個小空間讓訪客親身體會當時的情況。因為當時很多人怕還有餘震會將房子震垮，所以大家都聚集在公園裡搭帳篷。此外，我們還打算將一台舊車放入此展區。因為當時很多人沒辦法回家，只能在車上聽廣播來了解整個狀況。接下來是第二展區，災民的訪談。我們將第二展區布置成組合屋的概念，想讓訪客體驗當時災後重建的臨時避風港。在此展區，我們也會用影片紀錄災民的經歷，並且邀請他們來演講、分享整個事件的過程。使得整個觀影及分享過程更加身歷其境。而第三展區的話，將會放置一些當時因地基不夠穩固而倒塌的建築模型，以及其後來的重建模型，讓參觀者清楚地學習當時的災後重建過程。

Materials

The curatorial discourse makes visitors know that because of a certain motive, we want to return to the background and process of 921. Coming to zone 1, we present the cause of the earthquake and the disasters caused by it with the help of photos and text narratives. Then, we use some of the news, newspapers, and radio sounds at the time, as well as the setting of tents. It can create small spaces for visitors to experience the situation firsthand. Because many people were afraid that there would be aftershocks that would destroy the house, everyone gathered in the park to pitch tents. In addition, we intend to put an old car in this zone. Because many people had no way to go home, they could only listen to the radio in the car to understand the whole situation at that moment. Next, zone 2 focuses on interviews with the victims. We arrange zone 2 as a prefabricated building, hoping visitors can experience the temporary shelter that was constructed after the disaster. In this zone, we use videos to record the experiences of the victims, and invite them to give speeches and share the whole process of the incident, which makes the whole viewing and sharing process more immersive. In zone 3, there will be some architectural models that collapsed due to insufficient foundations at that time, as well as their later reconstruction models, so that visitors can clearly learn about the reconstruction process at that time.

體驗活動

To run or not to run

規劃地震體驗屋並加入VR，高度還原921大地震的景象，讓體驗者身歷其境。
We plan to make an experience room for earthquakes with VR technique, which highly restores the terrifying scene from the Ji-Ji earthquake, in order to make participants feel like they were in this tragedy in person. Besides, we have different experience rooms for various disabled. Take people who are visually impaired for example, we focus on the sounds which are picked out from some news clips and reduce the shaking of the earthquake room.

The twilight of hope

結束地震屋體驗後，我們希望透過歷史紀錄片完整呈現當時的災情和災民們是如何重建自己的家園，幫助遊客更深入了解與體會其失去一切及重建家園的掙扎與痛苦。
After finishing the experience room, we hope to represent how terrible the disasters were at that time and how victims rebuild their homes through a historical documentary. Visitors are allowed to acquaint themselves with the pain of losing everything and the struggles to rebuild their homeland.

結論

透過這個展覽，我們除了想讓大家了解921的事發經過，最重要的就是台灣的災後重建。雖然一些天然災害我們無法避免，但事後的重建卻讓我們受益匪淺。由這些天然災害的重建過程，凝聚我們對台灣的歸屬感，也讓我們從歷史的傷疤中學習到更有效的防範意識。最後，也想透過這個展覽提醒身為後代的我們，現在所享有的快樂都是在前人的歷史傷疤中重建而成的，所以應該知足。

Conclusion

Through this exhibition, we want to let everyone know the story of 921; however, the most important thing is the reconstruction of Taiwan. Although we can't avoid some natural disasters, we can benefit a lot from the reconstruction afterward. The reconstruction process of these natural disasters has caused cohesion and our sense of belonging to Taiwan, and allowed us to learn a more effective precautionary awareness from the scars of history. Finally, we also want to remind people as later generations that the happiness we enjoy now is reconstructed from the historical scar of those predecessors, so we should be content with what we have.
